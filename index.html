Welcome.

My name is Joey Blumberg. I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics at Colorado State University.

Research Interests

My interests broadly lie in water demand, conservation, and how climate change and population growth impact our food-energy-water systems. My current research examines how changing beliefs of water availability affects the adoption of conservation practices for Eastern Colorado farmers. My future work will invovle a climate change vulnerability analysis of Western US counties, specifically focusing on climate induced impacts on rural economies and agricultural sectors.

My previous research and master's thesis assessed the relative merits of coalescing unsupervised machine learning techniques with applied econometrics in the context of fruit and vegetable demand. Moving forward, I hope to further refine my technical expertise by delving into the growing machine learning literature.

Bio & Personal Interests

Before attending CSU, I received both a bachelor's and master's degree in Applied Economics at the University of Arizona. Between degrees, I worked in Tucson, AZ and Santa Fe, NM as an inventory manager and product photographer for a Native American and Southwestern art gallery. During my master's program, I spent a summer in Washington, D.C. interning at the Economic Research Service. I have worked as a research assistant throughout my graduate school career, resulting in a combined total of four years of economic research experience. I am currently a trainee in CSU's InTERFEWS Program, an interdisciplinary training, education, and research program focusing on food-energy-water systems in water-scarce, arid regions.

I like to spend most of my free time outdoors. I am an avid snowboarder and backpacker when the season calls for it. I am also an avid reader of fantasy and science fiction. The "Foundation" series by Isaac Asimov is my all time favorite fiction series.
