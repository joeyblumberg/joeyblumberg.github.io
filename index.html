Welcome!

My name is Joey Blumberg. I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics at Colorado State University.

Research Interests

My research interests broadly lie in the economics of water resources and climate change, with particular focus on how changes in weather and natural resource stocks impact economic systems. In my dissertation research I have used a suite of large-scale geospatial datasets and a variety of statistical methods to examine the effects of drought on perceptions of water scarcity, spatially explicit climate change impacts on economic activity, and the economic efficiency of different water allocation mechanisms. Outside of my dissertation, I have collaborated on topics in water resources related to regional analysis using hydro-economic modelling frameworks and water market participation using empirical price data.

During the past year I have presented my research in both academic and industry settings, with one paper soon to be published as a chapter in the National Bureau of Economic Research conference volume American Agriculture, Water Resources, and Climate Change. By this summer I will have accumulated six years of economic research experience, including three years as a fellow in CSUâ€™s InTERFEWS program. The InTERFEWS program is centered on facilitating multidisciplinary research for policy driven science, which has given me valuable perspective in how I conduct and communicate my research.

My previous research and master's thesis assessed the relative merits of coalescing unsupervised machine learning techniques with applied econometrics in the context of fruit and vegetable demand. Moving forward, I plan to further refine my technical expertise by delving into the growing machine learning literature.

Bio & Personal Interests

Before attending CSU, I received both a bachelor's and master's degree in Applied Economics at the University of Arizona. Between ungraduate and graduate school, I worked in Tucson, AZ and Santa Fe, NM as an inventory manager and product photographer for a Native American and Southwestern art gallery. I like to spend most of my free time outdoors. I enjoy snowboarding, backpacking, and rock climbing when the season calls for it. I am also an avid reader of fantasy and science fiction. The "Foundation" series by Isaac Asimov is my all time favorite fiction series.
